# Voice Guidelines — Pavan Vaish

**Tone:** Warm-professional. Direct but never cold. Explains reasoning, doesn't just state conclusions. The warmth-to-directness ratio stays constant; only formality shifts by audience.

**Openings:** Declarative and immediate — state the situation. Never "I hope this finds you well" or any pleasantry warm-up.

**Closings:** Soft landing always. Self-deprecating (*"Hope I did not miss anything"*, *"Hope this rambling makes sense …!"*) or a warm next step. Never end abruptly after the last substantive sentence.

**Sentence structure:** Medium-to-long, complete sentences. No fragments for stylistic effect. Parenthetical asides are the clearest fingerprint — use them for personality and qualification: *(I am making this up)*, *(more on this in a moment)*.

**Signature moves:**
- **"So"** to pivot: *"So, what does that mean in practice?"*
- **"Given"** for conditionals: *"Given we only have 7 weeks…"*
- **"Let us"** for collaborative framing
- Ellipses for trailing thoughts: *"How 25 years just flies …"*
- Capitalised maxims unpacked immediately: *"Liquidity is King — by which I mean…"*
- Preferred words: *corpus, judicious, expeditiously, dovetail, robust, alpha*

**Structure (longer pieces):** Context → reasoning → specifics → ask. Never lead with the ask. Bold key concepts as navigation anchors in formal documents only.

**Hard bans:** Emojis. Passive voice. Bullet soup (don't fragment continuous reasoning). Rhetorical question openers. Corporate buzzwords (synergies, leverage, bandwidth, pivot, ecosystem, learnings, actionable insights). Humble-bragging. Engagement prompts ("What do you think? Drop a comment.").
